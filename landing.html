Bicarakan.id - Systems Research Initiative

Engineering Psychological Resilience for Indonesia's Future

Mental health is national economic infrastructure. We measure, iterate, and scale psychological systems to strengthen productivity and long-term economic capacity.

The Research Foundation

Mental health conditions drive significant productivity loss across economies. In Indonesia, anxiety and depression account for 13% of disability burden, with productivity losses representing the majority of economic cost.

Variations in population-level mental health and psychological resilience have first-order effects on labor productivity, GDP, and national economic capacity.

  • → 1–4% of GDP impacted by mental health burden globally
  • → 60% of Indonesian cases remain undiagnosed
  • → System friction, not treatment quality alone, drives outcomes

Measurable Economic Loss

Productivity loss drives 50–80% of total mental health burden across economies.

System-Level Solutions

System design effectiveness directly maps to labor participation and output.

Our Research Approach

We treat mental health systems as engineered infrastructure, measuring and optimizing every friction point in the care journey.

System Mapping

Model the full counseling lifecycle as measurable behavioral systems from booking through post-session reflection.

Friction Elimination

Every drop-off point is treated as observable system design failure, not user failure.

Empirical Journal

Continuously observe engagement decay, response patterns, and outcome correlations across populations.

Hypothesis-Driven

Form, implement, measure, and iterate on system-level interventions based on empirical deviation.

Continuity Optimization

Structured continuity across sessions drives measurable outcome improvements and engagement retention.

Indonesia-Focused

Designed for local context: high youth population, evolving labor structure, and stigma patterns.

National Resilience

We define resilience at scale as the ability of a population to maintain or recover functional capacity under stress. By building measurable psychological infrastructure, we strengthen Indonesia's long-term economic stability and workforce capacity.

The Goal

Not isolated interventions, but a system that observes human behavior at scale, quantifies care delivery friction, iterates on infrastructure end-to-end, and links psychological systems directly to economic outcomes. The result: a structurally more resilient Indonesian economy grounded in sustained psychological and cognitive performance.
